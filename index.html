Bears are neat!

Bears are mammals that belong to the family Ursidae. They can be as small as four feet long and about 60 pounds (the sun bear) to as big as eight feet long and more than a thousand pounds (the polar bear). They're found throughout North America, South America, Europe, and Asia

They have nonretractable claws, short tails, and excellent sense of smell. They're typically solotary, except for mothers with cubs. There are eight species: Asiatic black bears(also called moon bears), brown bears(which include grizzly bears), giant pandas, North American black bears, polar bears, sloth bears, spectacle bears(also called Andean bears), and sun bears.

On average, bears can live up to 25 years in the wild and 50 in captivity. Six species, including the polar and giant panda, are included on the IUCN Red List as threatened or vulnerable
